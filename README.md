# Addressing healthcare disparities with social determinants of health (SDOH)

## Objectives of the project
This project's primary objective is to integrate SDOH data analytics into a centralised system to enable real-time, data-driven decision-making and intervention design using Excel, Power BI, and Power Automate. 
Through this, we aim to :
Integrate fragmented SDOH datasets into a unified platform to provide a comprehensive view of healthcare disparities.
Automate data collection and processing workflows using Microsoft Excel and Power Automate to ensure real-time, accurate reporting.
Develop interactive Power BI dashboards with geospatial and trend analysis to visually identify regions with the highest healthcare disparities.
Create a system for continuous monitoring of health equity indicators to ensure interventions are adjusted based on the latest data.
Enable collaboration among healthcare providers, policymakers, and analysts through Power BI online sharing.

## Significance of the project
A project focused on addressing healthcare disparities through the lens of Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) is highly significant for artificial intelligence, epidemiology, and health science, as it enables a more comprehensive understanding of how social, economic, and environmental factors influence health outcomes and disease patterns. 

In AI, integrating SDOH data improves predictive accuracy, reduces algorithmic bias, and supports the development of fairer and more effective healthcare models for risk prediction, disease forecasting, and resource allocation. 

In epidemiology, the project enhances disease surveillance, risk stratification, and understanding of population-level health inequalities by examining how factors such as income, housing, education, and healthcare access contribute to disease transmission and health disparities. 

In health science, the project promotes a holistic and preventive approach to healthcare by addressing the root causes of poor health outcomes, improving healthcare accessibility, supporting evidence-based public health interventions, and advancing health equity. 

Overall, the integration of SDOH into AI and public health research contributes to precision public health, informed healthcare policy, and improved population health outcomes.
